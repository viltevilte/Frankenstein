Frankenstein by Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley


Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein. Made by Viltė Vytaitė as a project for Text as Data course, taught at the University of Antwerp.

Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus, written by Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley, is one of the most celebrated works of English literature. At just 18 years old, Mary conceived the story of a scientist and his monster, in response to a challenge from Lord Byron to write a frightening story to distract from a disappointingly rainy holiday. Her story not only won the competition among the holidaymakers but also became one of the most iconic works of Western literature.

Although the initial story and early drafts were lost, the focus of this project - the two-volume version draft of 1816-1817 - is still extant. The readers of this edition are able to observe the process of the this story's edition as well as the collaboration of Mary's husband Percy B. Shelley: all the corrections and lexical changes Percy adds to his wife's work.

The project focuses on the chapter 7A of from Frankenstein's Bodleian MS. Abinger c.56 manuscript, which became the 5th chapter of the published ouvre. This digital edition includes all 10 pages of the chapter:
